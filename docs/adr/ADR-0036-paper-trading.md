# ADR-0036: Paper Trading Architecture

## Context

In M18, GQOS transitioned from historical backtesting to live, asynchronous Paper Trading. We needed an engine capable of interacting with live data feeds in real-time, executing signals without lookahead bias, and continuously updating a Shadow Portfolio in memory.

## Decision 1: Event-Driven Orchestration Trigger

We decided to trigger Alpha forecast generation exclusively upon receiving a `BarClosedEvent` rather than using a wall-clock scheduler.

* **Rationale**: If we used a wall-clock scheduler (e.g., cron job at 15:59:59), slight delays in market data feeds could cause the Alpha Engine to generate a forecast on incomplete or missing bar data. By strictly tying orchestration to the `BarClosedEvent` published by the adapter, we guarantee that the Alpha logic only executes when a full, confirmed bar of data is available. This maintains perfect parity with our backtesting loop logic.

## Decision 2: Paper Fill Logic (Queue and Wait)

When an `OrderEvent` (`ExecuteTradeCommand`) is generated by the Paper Trading Engine, the `PaperExecutionHandler` does **not** fill it immediately at the last known price. Instead, it places the order in a queue and waits for the **next** incoming `MarketDataEvent` (tick/bar) to fill it.

* **Rationale**: Forecasting at bar $t$ means using `close[t]` as the signal input. If we filled the resulting order instantly at `close[t]`, we would inject massive lookahead bias into the Paper Trading results. By queuing the order and applying slippage/commissions against the actual price of the *subsequent* tick, we simulate the realistic latency and friction of live order routing.

## Decision 3: In-Memory State for M18

For M18 Paper Trading, we allow the `AccountingEngine` and `PortfolioManager` to keep their states entirely in-memory, without forcing disk serialization.

* **Rationale**: M18's goal is to prove the asynchronous, real-time event flow and the validity of the fill logic. Production-grade persistence (e.g., Ledger database, crash recovery) is a distinct infrastructural challenge deferred to M19 (Live Trading).

## Decision 4: Daily Attribution Isolation

The `DailyAttributionEngine` parses all fills to emit a `DailyAttributionEvent` that clearly splits the gross Alpha PnL from Friction Costs (slippage + commissions).

* **Rationale**: In paper trading, knowing the net PnL is insufficient. We must be able to prove whether a strategy's edge is being completely eroded by execution friction. Explicit separation allows the research team to distinguish between "broken Alpha" and "bad execution."

## Status

Approved and implemented in M18.
