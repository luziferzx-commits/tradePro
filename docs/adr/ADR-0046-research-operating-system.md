# ADR-0046: Research Operating System

## Context
GQOS has completed its engineering phase (M1-M27). However, to transition into a true institutional Quantitative Hedge Fund, the platform requires a robust "Research Operating System" to track, validate, and store the intellectual property (Alphas) generated by the system. The Head of Quant Research mandated the creation of the Phase 0 Infrastructure to prepare for the Edge Validation Campaign.

## Decision 1: Research Archive (DuckDB/SQLite) over Simple File Storage
We transitioned from simply logging outputs to storing all Alpha generation attempts (both successful and failed) in an `IResearchStore` (defaulting to DuckDB/SQLite).
* **Rationale**: Research is an OLAP (Online Analytical Processing) workload. Storing 10,000 failed Alphas with their parameters and metrics enables future data-mining. If a new dataset is acquired, the "Cemetery" can be queried to resurrect Alphas that were previously discarded.

## Decision 2: Pluggable Edge Score with Robust Percentile Scaling
We deprecated Sharpe Ratio as the singular optimization metric. We implemented the `IEdgeScoreModel` which scores Alphas out of 100 based on a weighted combination of Sharpe, PBO, SPA, Execution Slippage, Capacity, and Stability.
* **Rationale**: Optimizing for Sharpe alone often leads to overfitted, illiquid strategies with low capacities. By optimizing the composite `Edge Score`, the Factory produces robust, tradeable portfolios. `Robust Percentile Scaling` was chosen over fixed bounds because market environments dictate relative performance (e.g., in a crash, a 0.5 Sharpe might be the 99th percentile).

## Decision 3: Hypothesis Registry & Feature Genealogy
We implemented the `IHypothesis` registry and `FeatureGenealogy` engine. Every Alpha must be tied to a human-readable hypothesis, and every Feature must track its dependent Alphas.
* **Rationale**: Alphas without a theoretical foundation are just statistical noise. The Hypothesis Registry enforces scientific method. Furthermore, if a bug is discovered in a feature (e.g., a look-ahead leak in RSI), the Genealogy engine can instantly flag all contaminated Alphas across the entire firm.

## Decision 4: Infrastructure Scope Lock
A hard scope lock has been placed on the architecture. No new infrastructure features (such as Alternative Data Integration) will be built until the Edge Validation Campaign completes.
* **Rationale**: Engineering must yield to Research. The platform must prove it can extract edge from existing OHLCV data before ingesting complex alternative datasets.

## Status
Approved and Implemented in Phase 0 Infrastructure Prep.
